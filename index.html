Start Tank @Chennai

PayPal India, in partnership with The Indus Entrepreneurs (TiE) Chennai Chapter, launched Start Tank, the startup incubator, at PayPal’s Chennai development center in 2013. The initiative seeks to nurture and support the creation of a new generation of technology companies by offering them not just initial infrastructure and mentorship, direction and encouragement, but also a chance to work alongside some of the best minds in the field. Start Tank, which is a part of the Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) program of PayPal, provides a cost and obligation-free environment for the selected startups to evolve.

Start Tank Chennai aims to be a world-class incubation center that nurtures world-class innovative ideas. In its first year, it seeks to support 3-5 innovative incubatee startups in the areas outlined in the ‘Eligibility section'. Come visit us @ PayPal India Pvt. Ltd., 6th floor, Futura IT Park, Block A, 334, OMR Road, Sholinganallur, Chennai, Tamil Nadu 600-119

 

Eligibility

  • Formally incorporated pre-angel/pre-venture funded seed and early-stage technology startups
  • Applicants selected through a joint selection process between PayPal and TiE Chennai
  • Startups with a significant tech centric component that can benefit from PayPal’s technical mentors and TiE’s business mentorship
  • Companies willing to commit full time to incubation

Assistance to Startups

Office area of 11,400 sq.ft. which includes a world-class boardroom, discussion room, and a state-of-the-art conference room to support a total of 5-8 companies (up to a maximum of 15 people each).
Technology mentorship – leveraging eBay Inc.’s global technology and product competency
Business mentorship – leveraging TiE and its seasoned entrepreneur pool
Access to soft mentorship in HR, technology trainings, talent acquisition best practices, team identification, leadership development, university relations Workplacelogistics such as cafeteria, transport, mail room, doctor’s room, mothers’ room, ATM, library, and fitness center.
